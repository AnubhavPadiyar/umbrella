About Umbrella
Built for the mountains.
Because the mountains don't wait.
A multi-hazard early warning system for the Indian Himalayan Region — connecting live satellite data to village-level disaster response.
The Problem

The Indian Himalayan Region — Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Sikkim — faces an accelerating cycle of disasters. Glacial lake outbursts, flash floods, forest fires, earthquakes. The data to predict these events often exists. What doesn't exist is a tool that connects that data to the ground — which village is at risk right now, which road is the last one open, how many minutes until the flood front arrives.

Umbrella is built to fill that gap. Not just weather alerts — ground intelligence for district-level response.

What Umbrella Does
🌧
Flood & GLOF Risk
Live 48hr rainfall from Open-Meteo API. Glacial lake travel times to downstream villages. IMD threshold classification.
🌍
Seismic Monitor
Live earthquake feed from USGS. M2.5+ detections within 350km. Village seismic zone classification per BIS IS 1893.
🔥
Forest Fire Tracker
NASA FIRMS VIIRS satellite detections. 375m resolution, updated every 3 hours. Village risk by proximity and forest cover.
📊
Village Vulnerability Scorer
15 villages across 6 districts scored using the NDMA Risk Framework. Live scores — not hardcoded values.
🗺
Live Hazard Map
5 layer toggles — GLOF, Flood, Seismic, Forest Fire. All live data, all on one map.
📄
PDF Situation Report
One-click situation report from live data. Village scores, rainfall summary, recommended actions. Formatted for government use.
