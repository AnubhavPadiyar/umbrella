Methodology
How Umbrella calculates disaster risk — explained for both field officers and technical reviewers.

Umbrella monitors 15 villages in the Uttarakhand Himalayan region that face risk from glacial lake outbursts (GLOF) and flash floods. Every village gets a risk score from 0 to 100 based on three things: how dangerous the weather is right now, how isolated the village is geographically, and how many people live there. The higher the score, the more urgent the need for action.

01
Live Rainfall Check
Every page load, Umbrella fetches the last 48 hours of actual rainfall from the Open-Meteo weather API for each district. High rainfall means flood risk is rising right now.
02
Geographic Risk
Each village has a fixed geographic score — how close it is to a glacial lake, how many roads lead in and out, and whether it has suffered a disaster before. These facts don't change with weather.
03
Score & Prioritise
The system adds all factors together into one score. Villages scoring 70 or above need immediate attention. 45–69 means watch closely. Below 45 is low risk under current conditions.
🔴 HIGH RISK
Score ≥ 70
Immediate action. Deploy NDRF. Prepare evacuation. Issue public alert.
🟠 MEDIUM RISK
Score 45–69
Elevated watch. SDRF on standby. Monitor rainfall every 3 hours.
🟢 LOW RISK
Score < 45
Normal monitoring. No immediate action required. Check daily.
Why should you trust these scores?
📡
Rainfall data is real — not estimated
Live 48-hour cumulative rainfall from Open-Meteo API, updated on every page load. Same data source used by meteorological agencies.
🏔
Glacial lake locations from ISRO NRSC
Glacial lake coordinates and travel times derived from ISRO's National Remote Sensing Centre inventory of Himalayan glacial lakes.
✅
Validated against Kedarnath 2013
When tested with June 2013 rainfall data (185mm over 48 hours), the formula correctly scored Kedarnath at 85/100 — HIGH risk — matching the actual catastrophic event.

Data Sources
📡
Open-Meteo API
Free, open-source weather API. 48hr cumulative precipitation_sum. Updated hourly. No API key required. Coordinates: district centroids.
🏔
ISRO NRSC — Glacial Lake Inventory
National Remote Sensing Centre inventory of Himalayan glacial lakes. Lake coordinates and GLOF travel times to downstream villages estimated from DEM analysis.
📋
NDMA Risk Framework
National Disaster Management Authority guidelines. Risk = Hazard × Vulnerability × Exposure. IMD rainfall thresholds: ≥115mm = HIGH, 65–115mm = MEDIUM.
🗄
Village Database (SQLite)
15 villages across 6 Uttarakhand districts. Static fields: population (Census 2011), travel time, road access routes, historical events. Hosted on Railway.
Known Limitations
District-level rainfall is used as a proxy — village-level gauges would be more accurate.
GLOF travel times are estimates based on river gradient and valley geometry — not hydraulic modelling.
Seasonal population surge (pilgrimage season) not yet factored in — Kedarnath and Badrinath populations can increase 10× in June–September.
Formula covers GLOF and flash flood only — landslides, earthquakes, and avalanches are not modelled.
Road access is treated as a fixed integer (1/2/3 routes) — actual road conditions change dynamically.
